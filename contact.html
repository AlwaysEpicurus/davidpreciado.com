About Me

I help people and teams get unstuck by turning complexity into clarity—and technology into something that actually works for the humans behind the screen.

I’ve worked across industries and business stages, from startup scrappiness to enterprise scale. Along the way, I’ve coached teams, built strategies, led product launches, and designed enablement that actually enables. I thrive in spaces where empathy, execution, and insight overlap—especially in industries where trust, timing, and clarity make all the difference.

My Core Values

Curiosity Over Assumptions

I stay open and ask better questions—because the real problem isn’t always the one people lead with.

Build Trust First

Long-term results are built on real relationships. I show up with consistency, transparency, and care.

Clarity is Kindness

The best strategies are simple, not simplistic. I help teams cut through the noise and focus on what matters.

Make It Make Sense (Then Scale It)

Growth should be useful. I build for today in a way that makes tomorrow easier, not harder.

5 Fun Facts About Me

  1. I’m lowkey obsessed with space and time. Not in an “I have a telescope” way—more like “how does gravity bend reality and why are we okay with that?” If you’ve ever fallen down an astrophysics rabbit hole at 2am… we’d probably get along.
  2. Cooking is my off-the-clock creativity. For me, it’s not about fancy plating or five-step reductions. It’s about turning leftovers into something craveable—and slowing down long enough to enjoy the process. It’s therapy… with garlic.
  3. I can turn pantry chaos into a meal worth bragging about. Give me a half-used bag of lentils, a rogue onion, and last night’s leftovers—I’ll make it work. I’ve got a sixth sense for flavor and zero fear of improvisation.
  4. I’m always reading—or listening—to something. Sci-fi is my sweet spot (especially the kind that messes with your head—think Ted Chiang meets Stephen King), but I’ve also got a soft spot for nonfiction that challenges how we live and lead (*Inner Excellence* is a recent favorite). My podcast queue is a rotating cast of comedy, business, news, and whatever else hooks me—usually four or five in the mix, always making room for one more.
  5. If I had a second career, I’d give this one away. Same work, same purpose—just for people who wouldn’t otherwise get access. I believe technology should empower everyone, not just the budget-approved.
